💡 Useful Japanese Etiquette Tip for Indian Exchange Students

When giving your speech at the Speech Festival, keep your hands respectfully down by your side or folded slightly in front of you. Bow politely when saying よろしくお願いします (Yoroshiku onegaishimasu) and ありがとうございました (Arigatou gozaimashita). The traditional greeting bow represents a high form of mutual respect and friendship between our two countries!

Lesson 1: Basic Self-Introduction

Part 1: The Essential Greeting Trio 🌸

These are the most important phrases in any Japanese self-introduction. Listen and repeat out loud!

Hello

こんにちは。

Konnichiwa

Nice to meet you (Beginning)

はじめまして。

Hajimemashite

Please treat me kindly (Closing)

よろしく おねがいします。

Yoroshiku onegaishimasu

Part 2: Introducing Name & Country 🌍

Let everyone know who you are and where you came from.

💡 My Name is... (わたしは ______ です)

Fill in your name in English or Katakana (like プリアンシャ) to practice pronouncing!

Result Phrase:

わたしは プリアンシャ です。

🗺️ I came from... (______ から きました)

Select your home country or target countries to hear the phrase in Japanese!

Result Phrase:

インド から きました。

Indo kara kimashita.

Part 3: Sharing Your Age (10 to 20) 🎂

To state your age, say: ______ さい です (______ sai desu). Select numbers 10-20 from our keypad!

Tap any age to practice pronunciation:

Age Selected: 15 years old

じゅうご さい です。

Juugo sai desu.

💡 Note: 20 years old uses the unique word "はたち" (hatachi) instead of "nijuu-sai"!

Lesson 2: School & Family

Part 1: My Grade/School Year 🏫

Tell your Japanese peers which school year you are currently in.

Select your current school grade:

Selected Grade: High School Year 2

こうこう にねんせい です。

Koukou ninensei desu.

Vocabulary Breakdown:
- ちゅうがく = Junior High School (Grades 7-9)
- こうこう = High School (Grades 10-12)
- ねんせい = Student of year...

Part 2: Introducing Your Family 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

To tell how many family members you have: かぞくは ______ にんです (Kazoku wa ______ nin desu).

How many family members in your home?

5 People (にん / nin)
Add Siblings (Optional Statement):

Your Family Sentence:
